FAQs

Which containers require a deposit or may be redeemed for a deposit?

If it's carbonated, we'll take it! Only beverage containers require a deposit and may be redeemed. The law states that a beverage means a "soft drink, soda water, carbonated natural or mineral water, or other nonalcoholic carbonated drink; beer, ale, or other malt drink of whatever alcoholic content; or a mixed wine drink or a mixed spirit drink."

All bottle, can and glass returnables must have a clearly legible barcode or they will not be accepted.

Can I bring beverage containers from other states or countries to Michigan to redeem for deposit?

Short answer: No. If Seinfeld has taught us anything, you'll end up in a mail truck with Newman pursuing your friend Jerry's stolen car.

Are the containers recycled after they are returned to the retailer by the consumer?

By law beverage containers are prohibited from disposal, thus they are recycled.

Do the bottles and cans need to be clean to be returned for deposit?

Technically a beverage container that contains nothing except the residue of its original contents is acceptable. We do request to reduce odors and nuisance problems from pests, return them dry and do not use the container for other materials.

What type of bags should I use to store the bottles in?

Bottles should be packaged in botl/hoardr bags only. Bags will be provided via mail once you register. If you require additional bags please submit your request 24 hours before your scheduled pick-up.
